Parent-Child Interaction Study in Coding Learning Settings

JUL 2018 - PRESENT

Photo Credits: DevTech Research Group

OVERVIEW

We analyzed Family Coding Days videos to measure interactions between parent and child while they play with different coding platforms.

MY ACTIVITIES

• Literature Research
• Bahavior and Utterance Analysis
• Study Setup and Conduct

CONTEXT

Research Project
DevTech Research Group, Tufts University
Advisor: Marina Umaschi Bers, Collaborator: Madhu Govind

RESEARCH QUESTIONS

• How can parent-child play sessions with graphical and tangible coding interfaces foster early literacy development?
• How do children ages 5-7 and their parents interact with graphical coding interfaces compared with tangible coding interfaces?

METHODOLOGY

We recorded videos of parent-child dyads collaborating on a coding project. We randomly assigned dyad to conditions: ScratchJr (graphical) and KIBO (tangible).
