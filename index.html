E-Mat

CHSD114: CHILD AND NEW TECHNOLOGIES

E-Mat (Emotion Mat) is an interactive robot for children ages 4 to 7 to aid their emotional development in a social setting.

Following an iterative design process, I worked with team members from mechanical engineering and child development to define project requirements, prototype and test our solution, and presented outcomes to faculty and students at our course's final project showcase.

StoryExplorer

DATA VISUALIZATION RESEARCH COLLABORATION

research

StoryExplorer is an interactive tool that visualizes a nonlinear narrative.

Collaborating with a graduate researcher from Visual Computing Group at Harvard University, I generated visualization sketches to reveal nonlinear narrative patterns in movie scripts, and designed user interface of Story Explorer.

We used Story Explorer to analyze 10 popular nonlinear movies and describe the spectrum of narrative patterns that we discovered, including some novel patterns not previously described in the literature.

VisMorn

INTERACTION DESIGN SPECIALIZATION

product designdevelopment

Vismorn is a goal-oriented morning alarm clock mobile application.

This is my first side project where I experienced the whole HCI design process from defining needs and pain points to creating a high-fidelity prototype.

Vismorn is for people who struggle to wake up at a scheduled time. Instead of setting a single time to wake up as other alarm clocks do, Vismorn provides two different times to set up, one for waking up and another for doing following task (e.g., go to work).
