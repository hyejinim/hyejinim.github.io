E-Mat

An Interactive Robot for Young Children's Emotional Development

Spring 2018 (4 months) | Team Project | CSHD141: Children and New Technologies course

My Role: Product Design, Child-Computer Interaction Design, Play Experience Design

Team: Kyle Paul, Riley Kolus, Robert Hrabchak, Meha Elhence

OVERALL

We created a E-Mat, emotional mat

PROCESS

Finding Problems & Opportunities > Defining Design Challenge > Brainstorming Ideas > Prototyping Technology and Designing Curriculum > Testing with Children > Discussion & Redesigning > Testing with Children > Discussion & Improvement

WHAT I DID AND LEARNED

Following an iterative design process, I worked with team members from mechanical engineering and child development to define project requirements, prototyped and tested our solution, and presented outcomes to faculty and students at our course's final project showcase.

1. PROBLEMS & OPPORTUNITIES

Developmentally Appropriate Technology
Open-ended content, gender-neutral technology, encouragement collaboration and physical movement
Emotional development

2. DESIGN CHALLENGE

"How can we make a developmentally appropriate technology for young children for emotinoal development?"

3. SOLUTION

Our solution was an interative robot that moves on an emotion map that allows children to recognize, express, and compare their own emotion to others while interacting with a robot and peers.

4. USER TESTING & REDESIGN
