OVERVIEW

E-Mat (Emotinal Mat) is an interactive robot for children ages 4 to 7 to aid their emotional development in a social setting.

RESEARCH QUESTION

"How can we make a technology for young children to aid emotinoal development and positive technological development?"

PROBLEMS & OPPORTUNITIES

Emotional Development (4-7 year olds)

Between ages 4 to 7 children reach emotional development milestones such as the improvement of understanding causes, consequences, and behavioral signs as children's language develops at this age.

Positive Technological Development

Many existing technologies and toys provide only limited content and are designed for girls rather than gender neutral. Applying thePositive Technological Development (PTD) framework, we wanted to provide open-ended content, gender-neutral technology, more communication and content creation opportunities.

E-MAT

Interactive Robot and Emotion Mat

Kids Putting a Face Card on a Robot

A Robot Moving on an Emotion Mat

Our solution is an interative robot that moves on an emotion map that allows children to recognize, express, and compare their own emotion to others while interacting with a robot and peers. So does the empathy expression while interacting with peers we utilize the five universal emotions: joy, sadness, anger, fear, and disgust. We color coded them with inspiration from the popular children's movie Inside Out.
The five colors are laid out on the e-mat and separated by binary levels of severity. Each shade represents very or a little bit of feeling each emotion.

Technology

E-mat is built on an Arduino platform which makes prototyping fast and effective. We gave Quinn wireless connectivity through the use of XP two point four gigahertz wireless communication modules allowing us to outsource some of the processing functions to an external computer and give us the opportunity to network Quinn with other IMET robots in the future.

When Quinn is given a destination emotion it automatically determines which color on the board is associated with that emotion and begins a journey to that sector. A color sensor mounted to Quinn's undercarriage helps tell it where it is on the board and lets it know when it has arrived at the destination. Quinn can follow lines on the map. Thanks to four infrared sensors under its body together these sensors feed into a proprietary control systems algorithm that steers Quinn around the E-mat.

Curriculum and Play

Kids Comparing Their Emotion Choice to One Another and Asking About Reasons

Kids Mapping Face Cards, Stories, or Their Own Drawing With an Emotion Map

Children are read short of fictional scenarios that they can relate to and then are prompted by a teacher or facilitator to select a face card that matches their feelings on the scenario. Quinn is a moving robot that children interact with. Providing a tangible vehicle for emotional expression. The face cards selected by each player are placed over Quinn's face. Programming it to move around the corresponding color on the mat. The main impact of the e-mat comes when children are able to compare Quinn's movements from their respective emotional responses. Answer questions such as "do you feel the same way", "do you feel different?", and "how can we tell?""
