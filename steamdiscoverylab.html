Overview

The STEAM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Art, and Math) Discovery Lab is one of Tufts enter for Engineering Education and Outreach (CEEO) research projects. The goal of the STEAM Discovery Lab is to provide educators with new and exciting ideas and resources for conducting hands-on activities in their classroom. The website provides teachers with inspiring lesson plans and activity ideas that they can tweak to best meet the needs of their students and subject area. The site also features activity ideas that combine different technologies in unconventional ways.


Challenge

As multiple authors (students and staff at the center) had been creating a lot of different types of content, the original navigation and website structure were not optimized for the current content. Also, the content produced by different authors was not always consistent. It made users harder to find content easily and effectively on the website as well as to visit the website again.


Solution

Over 10-week summer internship, I was responsible for redesining the website to make it more user-friendly. The most important thing was to make the content discoverable and accessible. I grouped content from scratch and redesigned information architecture. Through heuristic evaluation, I analyzed major usability issues of the previous website and improved usability by adding useful features such as filters and making important information visible. I also created user scenarios and optimied user flow based on that. Finally, I created illustration and updated graphics to make the website visually appealing.


Impact

The redesigned website was launched in Aug 2019. A demo session conducted after website redesign proved that the new site was a success; the website returning visitor rate increased and the time for finding information significantly decreased.
